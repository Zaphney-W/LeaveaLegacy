01 — Open Letter

Dear Reader,

Written after a year of stumbles, falls, and (hopefully) growth
It's your time, use it

Post AP Computer Science has been one of the toughest and yet easiest classes I have taken in my life. This may seem at first like a contradiction however I can assure you that at its core, Post AP manages to make this statement truthful. Post AP both opens doors to those who search for them and offers a shovel and a little patch of dirt to those who blind themselves to opportunities throughout the year. Trust me, you want to be holding a door handle and not a shovel.

I have chosen the shovel more times than I can count over the past year and although I have climbed out of my hole and started on the next plot of land, if I could go back I would have taken the doors into new ideas and challenges every time. Post AP is like a playground where you are given everything to learn and grow as a person but there is no one there to force you to use it. This dynamic forces a student to learn how to be disciplined enough to continue projects when there is no time crunch and to dive into new topics even if not for a grade. While I, along with most of the class, am still learning this skill, I am infinitely closer to grasping it than I was at the start of the year.

What I want to leave for the Post AP students of the future is this: We can’t pass judgement on something without experiencing it, so take this time to find out what you like, explore different fields, write a paper, read a thesis or two, find out what really drives you and then dive into that subject. This is what I failed to do, I failed to take a week or two to just discover like a little child. I still, as I am writing this, have only a little idea of what really drives me and this has hurt me in this class. We owe it to ourselves to learn as much as we can about life and Post AP Computer Science gives us the tools to dive deeper than we ever have before. Just make sure to use it.

— Zaphney Wooldridge, Class of 2027
02 — Survival Tips

What we wish
we'd known.

Tip 01

Start before you're ready

Waiting for the "perfect" idea is a trap. Your first idea will evolve — but only if you start working with it. Launch the imperfect version. Iterate from there.

→
Tip 02

Protect your deep work time

PostAP rewards sustained focus more than scattered effort. Block off time in your calendar like it's a non-negotiable appointment — because it is.

→
Tip 03

Document as you go

Don't wait until the end to record what you did. A photo, a quick note, a paragraph written the same day — these become invaluable when it's time to present or reflect.

→
Tip 04

Embrace the uncomfortable "middle"

Every project hits a phase where it feels messy and unclear. This is normal — it means you're actually doing something meaningful. Push through it.

→
Tip 05

Your classmates are resources

Seriously. Talk to them. Ask what they're working on. Their projects will reframe what's possible for yours. Cross-pollination of ideas is where PostAP magic happens.

→
Tip 06

Ask "why" before "how"

Before diving into execution, spend real time on purpose. Why does this project matter to you? That "why" will carry you through every frustrating roadblock.

→
Tip 07

Failure is data, not verdict

When something doesn't work, you've learned something specific. Log it. Adjust. The project you end with will almost never look like the one you planned — and that's a sign of growth.

→
Tip 08

Don't perform effort — do work

PostAP will see through "busy." Make things that actually challenge you. The grade (and more importantly, the growth) lives in the genuine stretch, not the performance of one.

→
Tip 09

Let your format surprise you

Your final artifact doesn't have to be a paper or a presentation. A zine, a short film, a working prototype, a map — think about what format would actually make your work sing.

→
"The blank page isn't an obstacle. It's an invitation. The sooner you stop waiting for instructions, the sooner you start doing work that actually matters."
— Collective PostAP wisdom
